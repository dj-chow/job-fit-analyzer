# Resume vs LinkedIn Hygiene Check Patterns

Over 90% of recruiters check LinkedIn during screening. Gaps between your documents look like lying or sloppiness. Both are deal-breakers.

## What to Check

### 1. Date Gaps
- Start and end dates for each role should match across documents
- Even small gaps (3 years on resume vs 2.5 years on LinkedIn) raise red flags
- Watch for date tricks used to hide job gaps
- **Risk level:** High. This is the most common honesty check recruiters run

### 2. Title Inflation
- "Marketing Manager" on LinkedIn when the resume says "Marketing Coordinator"
- "VP of Engineering" on one, "Senior Engineer" on the other
- Titles that could be promotions within a role should still match the real timeline
- **Risk level:** High. Seen as lying on purpose

### 3. Missing Roles
- A role that shows on LinkedIn but not the resume (or the other way around)
- Short stints left off the resume are common and fine. But if LinkedIn shows them, the gap is visible
- Freelance or consulting work on one document but not the other
- **Risk level:** Medium. May look like hiding something, or may be a smart resume edit

### 4. Metric Gaps
- Claiming 40% revenue growth on the resume but 75% on LinkedIn
- Different team sizes for the same role
- Clashing scale numbers (revenue, users, team size)
- **Risk level:** High. Kills trust in both numbers

### 5. Education Gaps
- Degrees not finished but listed as done on one document
- Different graduation dates
- Different majors or focuses
- Certs listed on one but not the other (minor)
- **Risk level:** High for degree completion gaps, Low for cert differences

### 6. Company Name or Description Differences
- Different company names (common with buyouts, but should be explained)
- Different takes on what the company does
- Clashing company size or stage details
- **Risk level:** Low. Usually harmless, but flag if it looks on purpose

### 7. Skills and Endorsement Gaps
- 50 skills on LinkedIn but only 10 on the resume
- Technical skills claimed on the resume but not shown in any LinkedIn role
- Certs claimed but not possible to verify
- **Risk level:** Low, but worth noting if the gap is extreme

## The Core Principle

The resume and LinkedIn should tell the SAME story at different zoom levels:
- **Resume:** A tailored story for a specific role, focused on what matters most
- **LinkedIn:** A broader story with more context and detail

Facts must match exactly: titles, dates, metrics, education. Framing can differ. The resume highlights what fits the target role. LinkedIn shows the full picture. But the raw data should be the same.

## How to Report Findings

For each gap:
1. State what conflicts (be specific: quote from both documents)
2. Rate the risk: High / Medium / Low
3. Give a fix: what to change and why
4. Note if the gap looks on purpose (tailoring) vs sloppy (oversight)

If no gaps are found, say so clearly. A clean hygiene check is a good sign. It makes the person more credible.
