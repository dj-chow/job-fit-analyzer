# Resume vs LinkedIn Hygiene Check Patterns

Over 90% of recruiters check LinkedIn during screening. Inconsistencies between documents are interpreted as dishonesty or carelessness — both are disqualifying signals.

## What to Check

### 1. Date Discrepancies
- Start and end dates for each role should match between documents
- Even small mismatches (3 years on resume vs 2.5 years on LinkedIn) raise questions
- Watch for date manipulation to hide employment gaps
- **Risk level:** High — this is the most common integrity check recruiters perform

### 2. Title Inflation
- "Marketing Manager" on LinkedIn when resume says "Marketing Coordinator"
- "VP of Engineering" on one, "Senior Engineer" on the other
- Titles that are plausible promotions within a role should still match the documented progression
- **Risk level:** High — perceived as deliberate misrepresentation

### 3. Missing Roles
- A role that appears on LinkedIn but not on the resume (or vice versa)
- Short stints omitted from the resume are common and acceptable — but if LinkedIn shows them, the omission is visible
- Freelance or consulting work on one document but not the other
- **Risk level:** Medium — may look like hiding something, or may be a deliberate resume tailoring choice

### 4. Metric Inconsistencies
- Claiming 40% revenue increase on resume but 75% on LinkedIn
- Different team sizes for the same role
- Contradictory scale indicators (revenue, users, team size)
- **Risk level:** High — destroys credibility for both numbers

### 5. Education Mismatches
- Incomplete degrees listed as completed on one document
- Different graduation dates
- Inconsistent majors or concentrations
- Certifications listed on one but not the other (minor)
- **Risk level:** High for degree completion discrepancies, Low for certification differences

### 6. Company Name or Description Differences
- Different company names (common with acquisitions — but should be explained)
- Different descriptions of what the company does
- Discrepancies in company size or stage indicators
- **Risk level:** Low — usually innocent, but flag if it looks intentional

### 7. Skills and Endorsement Gaps
- 50 skills on LinkedIn but only 10 on resume
- Technical skills claimed on resume but not reflected in any LinkedIn role description
- Certifications claimed but not verifiable
- **Risk level:** Low — but worth noting if the gap is extreme

## The Core Principle

The resume and LinkedIn should tell the SAME story at different levels of detail:
- **Resume:** Tailored narrative for a specific role, emphasizing relevant experience
- **LinkedIn:** Broader professional narrative with more context and completeness

Facts must match exactly: titles, dates, metrics, education. Framing can differ — the resume emphasizes what's relevant to the target role, while LinkedIn shows the full picture. But the underlying data should be consistent.

## How to Report Findings

For each inconsistency:
1. State what conflicts (be specific — quote from both documents)
2. Assess risk level: High / Medium / Low
3. Provide a recommendation: what to fix and why
4. Note whether the inconsistency looks intentional (tailoring) vs careless (oversight)

If no inconsistencies are found, state this explicitly — a clean hygiene check is a positive signal that strengthens the candidate's credibility.
