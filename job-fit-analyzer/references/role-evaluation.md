# Role-Specific Evaluation Criteria

How job fit evaluation differs across role types. Use this to calibrate your analysis to the specific function.

## Engineering Roles

**Primary evaluation axis:** Technical depth and system-scale indicators

What matters most:
- Specific tech stacks and languages (must match or be closely adjacent)
- System design experience at relevant scale
- Performance indicators: latency, uptime, throughput, data volume
- Code quality signals: testing practices, CI/CD, review culture
- Open source contributions or technical writing (bonus, not required)

What matters less than candidates think:
- Number of years (quality of experience matters more)
- Prestigious company names (unless the scale is genuinely relevant)
- Management experience (unless the role is explicitly EM)

Seniority signals:
- Junior: Can implement well-defined tasks with guidance
- Mid: Can own a feature end-to-end with minimal direction
- Senior: Can define the technical approach for ambiguous problems
- Staff+: Can set technical direction across teams and influence org-wide decisions

## Product Management Roles

**Primary evaluation axis:** Decision quality under uncertainty

What matters most:
- Evidence of making consequential decisions with incomplete information
- Shipped products or features with measurable outcomes
- Stakeholder alignment across functions (not just working with engineers)
- Ability to frame problems before jumping to solutions
- Customer/user insight that drove product decisions

What matters less than candidates think:
- Specific tools (Jira, Figma, Amplitude — these are learned in weeks)
- Technical depth (unless the role is explicitly Technical PM)
- MBA or PM certifications

Seniority signals:
- Associate/Junior: Can execute a well-defined product area with guidance
- Mid: Can own a product area and make independent prioritization decisions
- Senior: Can define product strategy and align cross-functional teams
- Director+: Can set vision across multiple product areas and influence company strategy

## Design Roles

**Primary evaluation axis:** Portfolio quality and process evidence

What matters most:
- Case studies showing end-to-end design process (problem, exploration, solution, outcome)
- Role clarity within team projects (what was YOUR contribution)
- Appropriate methods for the problem type (research, prototyping, testing)
- Visual and interaction quality in the relevant domain (B2B vs B2C, mobile vs web)
- Evidence of working within constraints (technical, business, timeline)

What matters less than candidates think:
- Tool proficiency (Figma vs Sketch is not a differentiator)
- Volume of work (3 deep case studies beat 20 shallow ones)
- Awards or followers

## Marketing Roles

**Primary evaluation axis:** Metrics-driven campaign and growth outcomes

What matters most:
- Revenue or pipeline metrics directly attributable to their work
- Experimentation culture (A/B tests, conversion optimization, attribution)
- Channel expertise relevant to the role (demand gen, content, product marketing, brand)
- Understanding of the buyer journey and how marketing influences it
- Cross-functional work with sales, product, or customer success

Seniority signals:
- Junior: Can execute campaigns and report on metrics
- Mid: Can own a channel or segment with budget responsibility
- Senior: Can set marketing strategy and connect it to business outcomes
- VP+: Can build and lead a marketing org and influence go-to-market strategy

## Sales Roles

**Primary evaluation axis:** Quota attainment and revenue metrics

What matters most:
- Quota attainment percentage (above 100% is the bar)
- Deal size and complexity relevant to the target role
- Sales cycle length and process sophistication
- Industry/vertical expertise if the role requires it
- Pipeline metrics: conversion rates, average deal velocity

Role-specific nuances:
- SDR/BDR: Pipeline generated, meetings booked, outbound volume and conversion
- AE: Revenue closed, deal size, win rate, sales cycle management
- AM/CSM: Net revenue retention, expansion revenue, churn prevention
- Sales Engineering: Technical win rate, POC conversion, deal support metrics

## Operations Roles

**Primary evaluation axis:** Process improvement with quantified outcomes

What matters most:
- Cost reduction, cycle time improvement, error rate reduction with specific numbers
- Process design and automation (not just process execution)
- Cross-functional coordination and change management
- Decision-making under constraint (budget, timeline, compliance)
- Evidence of building systems that scale beyond the individual

## Data Science / Analytics Roles

**Primary evaluation axis:** Technical methods + business impact

What matters most:
- Statistical rigor and appropriate method selection
- Specific ML/AI frameworks and techniques relevant to the role
- Business outcomes driven by their analysis (not just "built a model")
- Communication of technical findings to non-technical stakeholders
- Published work, open source, or portfolio projects (carries more weight than in other roles)

## Cross-Role Evaluation Notes

Regardless of role type, always evaluate:
- **Progression pattern:** Is the candidate growing in scope and responsibility over time?
- **Tenure distribution:** Mix of 2-4 year stints is ideal. Multiple sub-1-year stints raise questions.
- **Company-to-role context:** A PM at a 10-person startup did very different work than a PM at Google. Calibrate accordingly.
- **Recency weighting:** Experience from the last 3-5 years matters more than earlier career. But don't dismiss foundation-building roles entirely.
