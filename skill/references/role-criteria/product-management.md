# Product Management Scoring Criteria

## The Core Question
"Can this person own a product area, make good calls on what to build, and ship results with a team?"

## Scored Dimensions (5)

### 1. Product Ownership and Shipping (Weight: 25%)
Did they own a product or feature end-to-end? Did things ship due to their calls?

| Level | What It Looks Like |
|---|---|
| Strong (80-100) | Owned a product area with clear results. Set the roadmap, made scope calls, shipped to users. Has data that shows impact. |
| Solid (60-79) | Helped make product calls but shared the role. Or owned a small scope like one feature. |
| Partial (40-59) | Helped ship but in a support role (BA, project lead, scrum master). Gave input but did not own the calls. |
| Weak (20-39) | Worked near product teams but did not take part in product calls. |
| Gap (0-19) | No proof of product work or shipping in any form. |

### 2. Getting Teams Aligned (Weight: 20%)
Can they get teams to agree without being the boss? This is the PM super skill.

| Level | What It Looks Like |
|---|---|
| Strong (80-100) | Led work across 3+ teams. Solved clashes. Made calls stick with no formal power. Has clear examples. |
| Solid (60-79) | Worked across 2+ teams often. Some proof of shaping calls. |
| Partial (40-59) | Worked across teams but more as a helper than a driver. |
| Weak (20-39) | Mostly worked in one team or one group. |
| Gap (0-19) | No proof of cross-team work. |

### 3. User and Market Insight (Weight: 20%)
Do they make calls based on user needs, not just what others ask for?

| Level | What It Looks Like |
|---|---|
| Strong (80-100) | Ran user research (talks, surveys, usability tests). Calls tied to user insights. Used market or rival data to shape plans. |
| Solid (60-79) | Got input from users and turned it into product calls. Some proof of user-first thinking. |
| Partial (40-59) | Worked with user input but did not lead research. Reacted to asks rather than seeking out needs. |
| Weak (20-39) | Built what was asked for without asking if it was the right thing. |
| Gap (0-19) | No proof of user-facing work or market sense. |

### 4. Strategy and Data Thinking (Weight: 20%)
Can they frame problems, set success metrics, and make tradeoff calls with gaps in the data?

| Level | What It Looks Like |
|---|---|
| Strong (80-100) | Set product vision or strategy. Picked and tracked metrics. Made tradeoff calls and can explain why. Proof of saying "no" to things. |
| Solid (60-79) | Helped shape strategy. Tracked metrics. Some proof of tradeoff thinking. |
| Partial (40-59) | Ran plans set by others. Worked with metrics but did not pick them. |
| Weak (20-39) | Focused on doing the work without the big picture in mind. |
| Gap (0-19) | No proof of strategic thinking or metrics-led work. |

### 5. Technical Fluency (Weight: 15%)
Can they work well with dev teams? They don't need to code, but they need to speak the language.

| Level | What It Looks Like |
|---|---|
| Strong (80-100) | Has a tech background (CS degree, dev work) or deep proof of work with hard tech systems. Can talk about system tradeoffs. |
| Solid (60-79) | Works well with devs. Gets system limits. Has worked on complex tech products. |
| Partial (40-59) | Works with tech teams but leans on others for tech calls. |
| Weak (20-39) | Little tech contact. Mostly worked on business tools with simple tech. |
| Gap (0-19) | No proof of tech work or grasp of tech concepts. |

## Seniority Calibration

The same areas apply at all levels, but the BAR shifts:

| Level | What Changes |
|---|---|
| Associate/Junior PM | Owns features. Focus on doing the work. Still learning to lead. |
| Mid PM | Owns a product area. Balances strategy and doing. Can lead peers. |
| Senior PM | Owns a product line. Leads with strategy. Shapes leaders and cross-org work. |
| Director+ | Owns a set of products. Sets the path for many teams. Org-wide pull. |

When scoring, check if the shown level matches the role. A strong mid-level PM going for a senior role might score 65-75 even with great proof. The scope gap matters.

## Common Scoring Traps

- Don't give too much credit for "years." Eight years of the same work is weaker than four years of steady growth.
- Product Owners in Scrum teams sometimes did full PM work. Sometimes they just ran the backlog. Look at the real work, not the title.
- Startup PMs often did it all (PM + design + ops). Give credit for the PM parts but don't assume depth in any one area.
- Enterprise PMs often had a narrow scope but deep team dynamics. Don't dock points for narrow scope if the role needs those skills.
